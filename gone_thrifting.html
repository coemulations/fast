Gone Thrifting?

  • November 11, 2019
  • Sophia Rizzuti

Have you ever thrift shopped before? Know that feeling you get when you find a treasure amongst the hundreds of pieces?

With so many different types of people and personalities at Berkeley, you will quickly see that each Cal student has their own unique style. As we continue this fall season, clothes and style will continue to change as Berkeley kids are starting to bundle up for these colder months. However, shopping new retail can be a bit pricey, so we are going on a little journey through three funky thrift shops in Berkeley, where you can easily pick up some new fall favs. After investigating each one, we will reveal which store ranks number one amongst your fellow Berkeley students.

Buffalo Exchange

Located on Telegraph–but not unique to Berkeley–it offers some pretty cool clothing for a very low price. Who doesn’t love that? This thrift store opened in 1974, in Arizona, so you can rest assured that its thrift store reputation is tried and true. Buffalo Exchange constantly recycles new clothing into the store, most of which comes from people bringing in their own pieces to sell. Because of this, the racks are always changing, resulting in a mix of clothing from different time periods, varying styles, sizes, and colors. And, students will find pieces which will almost certainly not be seen on other students around Berkeley. Buffalo Exchange is the perfect place for a Berkeley student to shop on a budget–most pieces will be well under $20. However, the multiple racks, rows of shoes, and hundreds of bags will certainly suck many hours out of your day. You will definitely need to spend a decent amount of time in the store, because some pieces–well, maybe most–might not be your style. But, when you do find those hidden gems, the time that should have been spent on studying will be well worth it

One of our favorite Buffalo Exchange fall finds this season is this cheetah printed cozy sweater. Tan and brown–earthy, fall tones–mixed with the bold animal pattern makes for a subtle statement. Pair this with some 501 Levi’s and Doc Martens, and you will have a super quick, cozy, and fashionable fall outfit.

Mars Mercantile

Mars Mercantile, but don’t be fooled by its name. It lives right here in Berkeley on Telegraph. Mars offers a wide variety of clothing as well, spanning all the decades, including the 60’s, 70’s, 80’s, 90’s, and so on. Mars has a lot of clothing which is elaborate, aka bold statement pieces and many options that are perfect for costumes. Compared to Mars, Buffalo Exchange definitely has a larger amount of clothing that would be considered contemporary. But, don’t let that stop you from making your way inside. One of our favorite finds this season, from Mars, is this red corduroy jacket. Our friend Maddie paired this bold jacket with a white tank top, a pair of light wash 501 Levi’s, and a black accent belt, making for a super fun and stylish outfit.

Slash Denim

And lastly, thrift stop number three: Slash Denim, located in Elmwood. The bottom floor of this store is a little hidden secret. You walk in, and find yourself completely surrounded by jeans of all kinds–mostly vintage–in an underground setting. The Levi’s Surplus section, recognizable by a blue overhang above the walkin, is the perfect place to find a new pair of Levi’s, fit and tailored directly to your needs. Since 1979, this store has styled the city of Berkeley with the coolest pairs of jeans. Right when you walk in, you will be instantly greeted by the employees who will make sure you find a pair of jeans that you are looking for, and that they fit like a glove. However, this little store is not just vintage, as they offer new styles and brands as well, such as Dr. Denim and Naked and Famous. Okay, but here is the thing. 99% of the jeans here are priced at $75. Amazing quality, and a good pair of jeans, but definitely more expensive than your average thrift store. However, with the personalization of these jeans, they are well worth the price, and will be a staple in your closet all season long

Taking it to the Streets: The People’s Opinion on Thrifting

We hit the streets, aka Sproul, to hear about what people had to say about these three stores. The people spoke–opinions clearly favored Buffalo Exchange. One student said “Buffalo’s the best place to shop after you sell your clothes...totally guilt free because you spend money you have made!” Others said, “I love it because I can always find one of a kind pieces.” And, one student summed it up quite perfectly: “funky.”

So, looks like Buffalo is the winner. Hope you get shopping!
