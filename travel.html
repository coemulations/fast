Packing Tips so that You Can Thrive on Your Next Vacation

  • November 26, 2019
  • Eumi Son

2020 is just around the corner, and with a new year comes new adventures. One of the most stressful things about travelling is packing. You only have so much space in your suitcase to fill with so many outfits. As someone who has studied abroad before, here are some of the suggestions I wish I followed and mistakes that I’ve made so you don’t have to:

1. Look up the weather.

Google weather patterns for the time you’re travelling to your destination. Don’t bring Bahamas attire to the Netherlands in January. Or do, but at your own risk.

Milan in Summer.
London in the Fall.


2. The golden rule, and perhaps the most important one: Go expensive on basics and cheap on trends.

This applies to more than just your travel wardrobe, but it’s especially pertinent here. You’ll probably end up wearing your basics the most and when you’re traveling, you need basics that can handle the wear and tear. So if you do any pre-travel shopping, remember that basics go forever, while trends always cycle out. In fact, commit to packing more basics and bringing a few trendy items.


3. It’s all in the details

Growing up, my mom used to say, “Wear a different pair of sunglasses and you have yourself a brand new outfit.” With limited suitcase space, you might not have the room to pack an outfit for every single day or occasion. But if you decide to pair yesterday’s fit with silver jewelry instead of gold, wear a different belt, carry a different purse, or wear different sunnies, you can potentially cut your packing down in half.


4. For every pair of pants, make sure there’s that perfect shirt that goes with it.

In other words, make sure you bring your pieces with intention. Packing is all about optimization and saving space, so it might do you good to plan a lot of your outfits before hand.


5. You have to walk a lot more than a mile in your own shoes, so they better be good ones.

Choose a good pair of versatile, stylish, and comfortable sneakers. Traveling with uncomfortable sandals had to have been the biggest L during my trip to Barcelona. Exploring took the entire day and after walking miles and miles, I came back to our hostel to blisters and sores… not worth it! Besides the comfort, sneakers are stylish and can really tie together an outfit or tone it down.


6. Make athleisure a part of your wardrobe.

Athleisure is one of my all-time favorite trends and I’m happy to report that it’s here to stay. But more importantly, it’s comfortable. The luxury of being comfortable and stylish simultaneously is something we all should take advantage of. A pair of biker shorts, a graphic tee, a bomber jacket, and some sneakers could get you from the airport to your airbnb to the city center, no outfit change necessary.


7. Less is More.

Pack as light as you can. This is, by far, the MOST challenging thing for me, but through personal experience, I can vouch. I cannot tell you the amount of times I had to jump on my suitcase in order to fit that last sweater. During my Europe trip, my carry-on suitcase was so packed that the zipper popped open! Please-- and I say this with sincerity-- avoid the same mishap.


8. Always pack these in your carry-on:

A tote bag (easy to pack, environmentally friendly, and a cute accessory… need I say more?), an RFID pouch for your cards (which protects the chip on your credit cards from theft), space savers, a travel voltage adapter , lip balm, moisturizer, hand sanitizer, your passport, and your earphones.


9. Windbreakers and hats save lives.

Windbreakers are thin enough to carry around and cute enough to wear around, hats are great for bad hair days, hot and sunny weather, and topping off your outfit.


10. When in doubt, 2-2-2.

When you don’t know exactly how many of each item to bring, set a minimum of 2. Two tanks of different colors, two pairs of jeans, two dresses, contingent on how long you’re traveling for, of course! (And conversely, set a max number of each thing you can bring! It’ll really help you not go overboard.)


11. Get the skincare and makeup routine down to an exact science.

Sometimes, I only had 10 minutes to get ready or had to do my entire skincare routine on the train ride over. It forced me to perfect my glam in the shortest amount of time possible. Not only is this a time saver, but it’s also a space saver.


12. I DON’T KNOW WHO NEEDS TO HEAR THIS… BUT SOUVENIRS ARE NOT ALWAYS NECESSARY!!!

No, your mom does not need another candle from Bordeaux, especially if it takes up space and packs on suitcase weight. Keep it light by collecting and personalizing an extra postcard or buying a small keychain. Sometimes, it’s the thought that counts.


13. Get a disposable camera (or a film camera!).

One of my favorite things to do is to bring one disposable camera on a trip with me and take pictures throughout my trip. When I get the film developed after the trip is over, I have moments to look back on. And plus, the “film aesthetic” trend is back!


14. Keep a travel journal.

This is something I didn’t do while I was abroad. At the end of the day, all we have are memories. I would’ve loved to remember the full story of a time we ended up in some random bar in the South of France, but at the time I thought it was a story I would never forget. Keeping a journal gives you the chance to reflect and look back. It gives you the chance to connect your photos to memories.


15. Take tons of pictures and use this time to experiment with your wardrobe.

Express yourself and feel confident while traveling. Some of the most authentic experiences come when you decide to fully be yourself.


16. Last but not least, make sure you go into your trip with a heart of adventure.

You might be needing a change of pace, you might be fulfilling a life-long dream, or getting out of your comfort zone. No matter the reason, use this time to find yourself in some way, all while rocking killer outfits!

Here’s to hoping that next year is filled with travel, food, shopping, and wine.
